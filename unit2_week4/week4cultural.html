Discuss a time you had an emotional conflict with someone. What was it about? What happened?

About a month ago I got into a serious argument with my roommate. It sounds silly, but it all revolved around whether or not I was feeding my cat enough food.

What was the source of the conflict?

Part of it was timing. He was stressed out and said some things that were uncalled for, but I was also in a bad mood and I reciprocated. It was clear the argument wasn't really about the cat food, but more about how we were viewing eachother.

How did you handle that conflict?

After we shouted for a bit and we both calmed down, I confronted my roommate because I felt that our argument was too serious to start over something like cat food. I said that there was something bigger that we were arguing, and suggested we figure it out. My roommate declined, and we eventually forgot about it.

Did your actions make the conflict better or worse?

I'm not sure. I still feel like there's some animosity between us, but he's subletting his room right now so it isn't affecting me presently. I tried though.

If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?

Nothing. The argument wasn't bad enough to have a permanent effect on our friendship, but we at least got some things off our chests.

What did you learn from this experience?

Honestly? Didn't really learn anything. I guess we both got some catharsis out of it, but I wouldn't change a thing about how I acted.
